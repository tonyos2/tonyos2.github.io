Steps For Making Coffee

Ingredients
  • Coffee beans
  • Coffee grinder
  • Coffee maker
  • Measuring spoon
  • Stirring utensil
  • Airtight containers
  • Cleaning supplies
  • Mug or cup & Water
  • Kettle or pot
  • Flavorings or sweeteners (i.e. as milk, cream, sugar)
  • Optional: brewer, such as an espresso machine.

> Procedure

Choose your coffee beans: You want the most fresh, best, and recently roasted beans for the best flavor!
Grind the coffee beans with a coffee grinder. Use a coffee machine/maker and obtain a fine powdery grind.
Find the appropriate amount of coffee. Generally, use 1-2 tablespoons of coffee per 6 ounces of water.
Fill a kettle/pot water. The quality of the water affects the taste of the coffee. Use the best available!
Boil your water. As a rule of thumb, you want the water to be heated to a temperature around 200Â°F.
While the water is heating, prepare your coffee maker. Clean, fill, set up as necessary and appropriate.
Pour water with desired temperature over the coffee grounds. Use a slow, circular motion for ideal results!
Wait and let the coffee brew. This time can vary, so find the ideal time for your brew.
After the coffee has finished brewing, remove any filter or grounds from the coffee maker.
If using a French press, push down on the plunger to separate the coffee grounds from the brewed coffee.
Pour the brewed coffee into your mug or carafe.
Add any desired flavorings or sweeteners, such as milk, cream, sugar, or flavored syrups.
Stir the coffee to blend the flavors correctly. Use a spoon or other stirring utensil.
Taste the coffee and adjust the flavorings if necessary. Make sure to write down your preferred ratios.
Enjoy your coffee!
Store leftover coffee in airtight container in the fridge. It can be reheated later.
Clean your equipment. This prevents build-up and ensure a healthy lifestyle.
Experiment with other brewing methods, flavors, and and coffee blends to find your favorite coffee!
Consider investing in more equipment, such as a burr grinder or a French press for results.
Lastly, ask others for advice or recommendations. They can offer valuable insight for coffee!
